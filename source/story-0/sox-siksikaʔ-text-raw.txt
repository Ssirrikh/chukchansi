Hawitmi Siksika’ Limeek’ata’
How Stinkbug Became Black

Sox himichta’ ’oshto wa’aw shoyxow.
Skunk made fire in a deep hole.

Mi’in ’ama’ panaata’ Siksika’.
Then came Stinkbug.

Woodiyga, wilta’ Sox.
“Dance”, said Skunk.

Namix, woodiyga. Mi’in gidwiyag ’oshtow, hatimta’ Sox.
“Friend, dance. And then go around the fire,” sang Skunk.

Mi’in ’ama’ Sox ’utuyta’ Siksika’an ’oshtow.
And then Skunk pushed Stinkbug in the fire.

Wa’ henew ’adil’, Siksika’ t’ulunta’.
Way down there, Stinkbug burned up.

Wa’ ’ama’ ’adil’, ’ama’ ’oshit shaapinta’.
Then, way down there, the fire went out.

Mi’in ’ama’ Siksika’ tishta’, meejinmi limeek’am’.
Then Stinkbug came out, and he turned black.

’Ohom’ may’ huuda’an’ hawitmi hoyul’ Siksika’, ch’uyu’ da’ t’ulunta’.
We don’t know how Stinkbug was alive, even though he was burned.



Source:
Gamble, Geoffrey (editor). 1994. Yokuts Texts. Native American Texts Series. Mouton de Gruyter: Berlin and New York.
